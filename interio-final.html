Problem
Many people face uncertainty when trying to visualize how new furniture, decor, and color schemes will actually look in their home environment before making purchases. This leads to frustration and potential regret after buying items that don't work out as expected in the real space.

User journey

We outlined major scenarios where users interact with the furniture buying service, including initial exploration, furniture customization, and making purchase decisions to understand their journey and major pain points. We created two user journeys based on the following archetypes:
1. The New Apartment Owner
Alex has recently moved into a new, empty apartment in the city due to a job relocation. They are starting from scratch in terms of furnishing the apartment.
2. The Apartment Redecorator
Samantha is looking to refresh the look of her well-lived-in apartment by adding new pieces and perhaps replacing some of the old furniture.

Insights
Visual Mismatch Concerns
Quality Uncertainty
Trust and Credibility

There is often a discrepancy between how products appear on screen and in reality, particularly regarding size, scale, and color. This can lead to dissatisfaction and high return rates.

Customers express concerns over the inability to assess the quality of materials and craftsmanship through online images alone. The tactile experience of furniture shopping is missing, which can lead to uncertainty about product quality and durability.

Building trust online is crucial. Customers look for social proof through reviews and ratings to gauge the credibility of an online furniture store. They are more likely to buy from a website with positive reviews and clear, detailed product descriptions.

Return Process Anxieties
Decision Fatigue
Environmental Considerations

The return process for online furniture purchases can be complicated and costly. Consumers are often worried about the feasibility of returning large items if they are not satisfied, which can influence their buying decisions.

With the vast number of options available online, customers can experience decision fatigue. While having many choices is initially appealing, it can become overwhelming, making it hard to make a final decision.

More consumers are considering the environmental impact of their purchases. This includes preferences for furniture made from sustainable materials and concern over the carbon footprint associated with the delivery of large items.

User Testing
Hi-fi Wireframes

Following that, we developed high fidelity prototypes and conducted thorough usability testing to refine user experience.

1. Creating a project
No Clear Room Indicators

Users found it challenging to navigate the room selection settings due to the absence of distinct visual cues for different room types such as the living room or bedroom.

2. Adding furniture
Cluttered Interface

The layout of the bottom and side bars used for adding furniture was cumbersome, leading users to desire a more spacious screen display for easier navigation and visibility.

3. Product customisation
No product details

Users expressed a need for more detailed information on pricing, materials, and sustainability features of the furniture, which were not provided in the app.

4. Furniture placement
Misplaced Adjustment Controls

The control dialog box for adjusting the chair was positioned too far from the chair itself, causing confusion among users about its function.

Final Screens
Starting a project
Begin a new design project by scanning your room to create a digital replica where you can experiment with furniture placement and interior design.
AI Tool
AI-powered suggestions and recommendations for furniture placement based on your room dimensions, style preferences, and functional needs, enhancing the design process with intelligent insights.
Placing an object
Seamlessly place furniture and decor items within your virtual room by simply tapping and dragging them into position, allowing you to visualize how they fit within your space in real-time.
AR Rotation
Rotate and adjust furniture items in augmented reality to find the perfect placement and orientation, ensuring they harmonize with your room's layout and aesthetics from every angle.
Checkout
Finalize your design selections and seamlessly transition to the checkout process to purchase the furniture and decor items directly through the app, streamlining the entire interior design and shopping experience.

Takeaways
  • The integration of AR technology demonstrated that accuracy in visualization is vital for user satisfaction. Learning to balance technological capabilities with user expectations was a critical part of the development process.
  • Feedback helped identify usability issues and validate design decisions throughout the development phases.
  • The challenge of simplifying complex AR technology for everyday users without losing functionality was significant. This project highlighted the importance of intuitive design and easy navigation to enhance user adoption and engagement.
